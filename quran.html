Support of Khadijah RA

After this overwhelming experience, Muhammad ﷺ returned home trembling and sought comfort from his beloved wife Khadijah RA. She immediately believed in him and provided unwavering support during these crucial early days.

Khadijah RA took him to her cousin Waraqah ibn Nawfal, a learned Christian scholar who confirmed that Muhammad ﷺ had indeed encountered the same angel that came to Moses (Musa) AS, and that he was destined to be a prophet.

"By Allah, Allah will never disgrace you. You keep good relations with your kith and kin, help the poor and the destitute, serve your guests generously and assist the deserving calamity-afflicted ones."

- Khadijah RA to her husband

The Mission of Guidance

Divine Guidance

The Quran

Universal Message

His message was not limited to Arabs but was meant for all of humanity until the Day of Judgment.

The Miracle of the Qur'an

Literary Excellence

The Qur'an's linguistic beauty and literary excellence surpasses all Arabic literature. Its unique style, rhythm, and eloquence remain unmatched to this day.

Scientific Accuracy

The Qur'an contains numerous references to natural phenomena and scientific facts that were unknown to humanity 1400 years ago but have been confirmed by modern science.

Universal Guidance

The Qur'an addresses all aspects of human life - spiritual, moral, social, economic, and legal - providing timeless guidance for all times and places.

Preserved Word

Allah has promised to preserve the Qur'an, and it remains exactly as it was revealed, unchanged and uncorrupted for over 1400 years.

The Living Miracle

Today, the Qur'an is the most widely read and memorized book in the world. Millions of Muslims have memorized it completely (known as Huffaz), ensuring its preservation in both written and oral form.

The Qur'an continues to guide over 1.8 billion Muslims worldwide, providing spiritual comfort, moral guidance, and practical solutions for life's challenges. Its teachings on justice, compassion, knowledge, and social responsibility remain as relevant today as they were 1400 years ago.

"Indeed, it is We who sent down the Qur'an and indeed, We will be its guardian."

- Quran 15:9
