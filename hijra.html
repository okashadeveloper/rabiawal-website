The Great Hijrah

Migration to Madinah - الهجرة المباركة

"If you do not aid him - Allah has already aided him..."
- Quran 9:40

A Journey That Changed History

The Hijrah (migration) from Makkah to Madinah in 622 CE marked the beginning of the Islamic calendar and represents one of the most significant events in Islamic history.

Why the Migration?

The persecution of Muslims in Makkah had reached unbearable levels. The Quraysh tribe had imposed a social and economic boycott on the Muslim community, making life extremely difficult for the believers.

After 13 years of calling people to Islam in Makkah, Allah commanded the Prophet ﷺ to migrate to Madinah, where a group of people had already embraced Islam and pledged to protect and support him.

The Assassination Plot

The Quraysh leaders decided to kill the Prophet ﷺ and chose representatives from different tribes to carry out the murder collectively, so no single tribe could be blamed.

The Historic Journey

The Escape

On the night of the migration, Ali RA slept in the Prophet ﷺ's bed to deceive the assassins. The Prophet ﷺ left his house unseen, protected by Allah's miracle.

Abu Bakr's Companionship

Abu Bakr RA accompanied the Prophet ﷺ on this dangerous journey, showing his unwavering loyalty and dedication to Islam and its messenger.

Cave of Thawr

They hid in the Cave of Thawr for three days. A spider's web and bird's nest at the cave entrance misled the search parties, showing Allah's protection.

Divine Guidance

Throughout the 8-day journey, Allah protected them from capture despite the high bounty placed on the Prophet's ﷺ head by the Quraysh.

Welcome in Madinah

The people of Madinah (then called Yathrib) welcomed the Prophet ﷺ with great joy and enthusiasm. Young girls sang songs of welcome, and the entire city was filled with celebration.

The Prophet ﷺ first stayed with Abu Ayyub al-Ansari RA while the first mosque (Masjid an-Nabawi) was being built. This mosque would become the center of the new Islamic community and state.

"Tala'a al-Badru 'alayna, min thaniyyat al-wada'..." (The full moon has risen over us, from the valley of Wada')
- Welcome song of the people of Madinah

The Significance of Hijrah

Historical Impact

  • Beginning of the Islamic calendar (Hijri calendar)
  • Establishment of the first Islamic state in Madinah
  • Brotherhood between Muhajirun (migrants) and Ansar (helpers)
  • Foundation for Islamic law and governance

Spiritual Lessons

  • Trust in Allah's protection and guidance
  • Sacrifice for the sake of faith
  • Unity and brotherhood among believers
  • Sometimes leaving is better than staying

Divine Promise Fulfilled

"Indeed, he who imposed upon you the Qur'an will surely restore you to a promised destination."

- Quran 28:85

This verse, revealed during the Hijrah, promised that Allah would return the Prophet ﷺ to Makkah – a promise fulfilled during the conquest of Makkah.
