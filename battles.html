Battles for Truth and Justice

The battles fought during the Prophet's Ô∑∫ lifetime were not wars of conquest but defensive actions to protect the Muslim community and preserve the freedom to worship Allah.

Battle of Badr

üìÖ 17 Ramadan, 2 AH (624 CE)

The Battle of Badr was the first major battle between the Muslims and the Meccan Quraysh tribe. Despite being vastly outnumbered (313 Muslims vs 1000 Meccans), the Muslims achieved a decisive victory.

This battle demonstrated Allah's support for the believers and marked a turning point in early Islamic history. The victory boosted the morale of the Muslim community and established their reputation as a formidable force.

Divine Assistance

"Indeed, Allah had given you victory at Badr when you were few in number." - Quran 3:123

Battle of Uhud

The Challenge

Fought in 3 AH (625 CE), this battle tested the Muslims' resolve when they faced initial setbacks due to the archers leaving their positions.

Lessons Learned

Despite the challenges, this battle taught important lessons about discipline, following orders, and trusting in Allah's wisdom.

The Prophet's Injury

In this battle, the Prophet Ô∑∫ was injured when a stone hit his face, breaking his tooth. This showed his humanity and the fact that he shared in the struggles of his companions. His response was to pray for his enemies' guidance rather than curse them.

Battle of the Trench (Khandaq)

5 AH (627 CE)

Also known as the Battle of Ahzab (The Confederates), this was a 27-day siege of Madinah by a coalition of Arabian tribes totaling around 10,000 warriors.

On the advice of Salman al-Farisi RA, the Muslims dug a trench around Madinah, a military tactic unknown to the Arabs at that time. This innovative defense successfully protected the city and demoralized the attacking forces.

Islamic Rules of War

The Prophet Ô∑∫ established ethical guidelines for warfare that were revolutionary for their time

Prohibited Actions
